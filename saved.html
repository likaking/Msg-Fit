Add Data Schema
Whats does each word in each line of your data represent? e.g. The first word represent name,the second word represent email and so forth

Seperate each tag with a comma. Click the buttons above to append tags to the data schema area


*Please note each data must follow a uniform pattern*

The default data schema is: <Next of kin>,<Next of kin>,<Next of kin>,<Next of kin>,<Next of kin>

A data schema will enable the system know the arrangement format your data. This system has a defined data format, what happens if you have a diffrent format? You will get mix of data.

Lets assume this is what each line of your data look like:

Grace Amadi, graceAm@gmail.com, No. 25 Queens Estate London, Airtel Africa, 17/1/1994
Wendy Brown, Brown@gmail.com, No. 5 Brown Estate New York, Nike, 17/1/1991
Alexander Anderson, Anderson@gmail.com, No. 2 Free Country Berlin, Airtel Africa, 17/1/1995
Sarah Kofo, mrsKoffo@gmail.com, No. 80 Greater Accra Accra, Bank of Ghana, 17/1/1985
Stephanie Sutherland, Sutherland@gmail.com, No. 33 golden road, Paris, Channel, 17/1/1981
etc.

The data before the first comma in every line is a "name", the data before the second comma is an "email", the data before the third comma is an "address", the data before the forth comma is "where the induvidual is employed", and the last but not the least is "date of birth".

Here is what the date schema will look like: <name>,<email>,<address>,<company>,<date of birth>

*Works with the <date> tag*

Here is a simple illustration to guide you: "I will attend to 100 ("The number you typed in the first text field") people on each giving date ("The dates, seperated by comma you typed in the second text field") I have entered in the text field."

Allocate different time to various people. For e.g. If you will spend 10 minutes on each applicant and you have to screen 50 applicants for the whole day. If the Screening will time starts by 9 am, Instead of inviting all the applicants to crowd your office from 9 am till you are done. Here is a simple illustration. Lets assume the screening time is 9 am, the first person will be screened from 9:10am to 9:20am. There will also be a five mimutes break after each screening session. You can allocate 9:10 am to the first person, then allocate 9:25 am to the next person, 9:40am the the next person and so forth.
